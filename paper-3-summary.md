📄 Paper 3: Systems to Model the Personalised Aspects of Microbiome Health and Gut Dysbiosis (2022)

Journal: Progress in Molecular Biology and Translational Science
Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0098299722000607

What this paper is about

Why is everyone's microbiome different? This paper systematically goes through all the factors that shape your personal microbiome composition.

Key takeaways


Genetics — your DNA influences which microbes can colonise your gut
Birth mode — babies born vaginally vs. via C-section have measurably different microbiomes from day one
Geography — where you grow up shapes your microbiome significantly
Diet — one of the most powerful and modifiable factors
Antibiotics can permanently alter microbiome composition even after a single course
Environmental exposures — pollution, stress, and sleep patterns all play a role
"Gut dysbiosis" = when your microbiome becomes imbalanced, which is linked to diseases ranging from IBD to depression


Why this matters for my project

This explains why a one-size-fits-all solution can never work. My platform's whole premise is that individual variation must be accounted for — this paper lists exactly what drives that variation.
