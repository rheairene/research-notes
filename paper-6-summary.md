📄 Paper 6: The Long-Term Genetic Stability and Individual Specificity of the Human Gut Microbiome (Cell, 2021)

Journal: Cell
DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2021.03.024
Link: https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(21)00383-9

What this paper is about

Hard scientific proof that your microbiome is uniquely yours and stays that way over time. The researchers followed 338 people for 4 years and tracked how their microbiomes changed.

Key takeaways


Your gut microbiome is individual-specific — meaning it looks more like your own microbiome from 4 years ago than it looks like anyone else's microbiome today
Researchers developed a "microbial fingerprinting" method that could identify whose microbiome sample belonged to whom with up to 85% accuracy after 4 years and 95% accuracy after 1 year
The genetic makeup of microbes is more stable over time than their abundance levels
Microbiomes with higher diversity at baseline are more stable in the long term
They found 100 associations between changes in microbiome and changes in human health phenotypes over time
Concerning finding: antibiotic resistance genes increased over time, linked to meat consumption


Why this matters for my project

This is the scientific backbone of the personalisation argument. If microbiomes were random or interchangeable, personalised interventions wouldn't make sense. This paper proves they're not — they're stable, unique fingerprints that carry real health information.
