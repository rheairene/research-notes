Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1038/s44321-024-00149-4

What this paper is about

There's a gap between what we know about microbiomes and what we can actually do about it in a clinical setting. This paper argues that to close that gap, we need to move from trial-and-error microbiome interventions to rational, precise medical approaches — starting with better diagnosis.

Key takeaways


Thousands of studies link the microbiome to disease, but very few real-world interventions have succeeded
The problem isn't lack of knowledge — it's lack of a systematic framework for translating knowledge into treatment
You have to properly diagnose the microbiome problem before you can engineer a solution
Nutrients, microbes, and toxins we're exposed to throughout life continuously reshape the gut
Microbiome composition links to health from birth all the way to old age
