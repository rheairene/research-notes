📄 Paper 5: Microbiome-Based Therapeutics (2022)

Journal: Nature Reviews Microbiology
DOI: 10.1038/s41579-021-00667-9
Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41579-021-00667-9

What this paper is about

A comprehensive review of all the ways scientists are trying to turn microbiome knowledge into actual medicines and therapies. Covers the full spectrum from FMT to designer bacterial consortia.

Key takeaways


Live Biotherapeutic Products (LBPs) — living microbes administered as medicines (the next generation beyond standard probiotics)
Defined microbial communities — precisely chosen combinations of bacterial strains with known therapeutic effects
Metabolite therapies — instead of giving someone bacteria, you give them the specific chemical compounds those bacteria produce
FMT — currently most clinically proven for C. difficile infection; being explored for IBD, metabolic disease, and neurological conditions
Machine learning is increasingly being used to predict which microbiome interventions will work for which patients
Regulatory and manufacturing challenges are still major barriers to bringing these therapies to market
