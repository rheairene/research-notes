📄 Paper 2: Engineering the Gut Microbiome (2023)

Journal: Nature Reviews Bioengineering
DOI: 10.1038/s44222-023-00072-2
Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s44222-023-00072-2

What this paper is about

This is essentially the technical roadmap for how scientists are actually engineering gut microbiomes right now. It covers multiple approaches, from genetic modification of individual microbes to transplanting entire microbial communities.

Key takeaways


Engineered probiotics — bacteria genetically modified to produce specific therapeutic compounds inside the gut
CRISPR approaches — using gene editing tools to modify microbial DNA precisely
Bacteriophages — viruses that target specific harmful bacteria; can be used as precision tools instead of broad-spectrum antibiotics
Synthetic microbial consortia — designing a whole team of microbes that work together, rather than relying on a single strain
Fecal Microbiota Transplantation (FMT) — transferring gut microbes from a healthy donor to a sick patient; currently the most clinically validated approach
Engineering a single microbe is often not enough — you need a collaborative network ("keystone consortium") to create lasting change


Why this matters for my project

My platform proposes custom probiotic/prebiotic formulations. This paper confirms that single-strain solutions are insufficient and that the future is in engineered consortia — which validates the direction of my idea.
