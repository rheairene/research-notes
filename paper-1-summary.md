📄 Paper 1: Utilisation of the Microbiome in Personalised Medicine (2024)

Journal: Nature Reviews Microbiology
DOI: 10.1038/s41579-023-00998-9
Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41579-023-00998-9

What this paper is about

Two people can have the same genes and still get completely different diseases, or respond differently to the same treatment. This paper argues that a big reason for that is the microbiome — the community of bacteria, viruses, and fungi living in and on your body. Each person's microbiome has a unique "signature," kind of like a fingerprint, and that signature influences how diseases develop and how well treatments work.

Key takeaways


The microbiome isn't just about digestion — it affects immunity, mental health, and even how your body responds to drugs
Because microbiomes are person-specific, population-level treatments (one drug for everyone) often fail
Microbiome data could be used for early disease detection in at-risk individuals
Modulating someone's microbiome could become a personalised, targeted treatment — not just a general probiotic


Why this matters for my project

This is the core scientific justification for why a personalised microbiome platform makes sense. Generic probiotics fail because they ignore individual biological variation. This paper proves that the variation is real and measurable.
